WHAT IS AHARADA KAUSHAL KUMBH?

Aharada Kaushal Kumbh is a dynamic AI & Machine Learning innovation sprint designed to bring together aspiring data scientists, ML engineers, and industry-minded innovators.

More than just a hackathon, it is a platform for creativity, problem-solving, and real-world application of artificial intelligence. Participants work on real datasets, build predictive models, and transform ideas into impactful solutions.

This event provides students with an opportunity to bridge the gap between academic knowledge and industry needs. By applying analytical thinking, machine learning techniques, and data-driven decision making, participants showcase their technical “Kaushal” (skills) on a competitive stage.

At Aharada Kaushal Kumbh, you don’t just build models — you build solutions that shape the future of AI.

THE COMPETITION

Aharada Kaushal Kumbh is an Inter-University AI & ML Competition designed to encourage innovation, collaboration, and practical problem solving among students.

Participants from different universities will compete by presenting data-driven solutions to real-world challenges, demonstrating their technical expertise, creativity, and teamwork.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who can participate in this hackathon?

Any college student who is passionate about Data Analytics, Machine Learning, and AI. This is an Inter-University challenge hosted by Aharada Education / (IIMT University), so beginners and experts from all universities are welcome!

Is there any registration fee?

No, participation in Aharada Kaushal Kumbh is completely FREE!

What is the required team size?

Each team must consist of 3 to 5 members, including the team leader.

What should we bring to the event?

Bring your college ID card, your laptop, chargers, and any specialized hardware you plan to use for your ML project.

Are we allowed to use AI tools like ChatGPT or GitHub Copilot?

Yes, AI tools are allowed under our AI Policy! However, your core problem-solving logic and the overall originality of the project must be your own.
